A country of 9.9 million people, with no significant natural resources, surrounded by conflict zones — and yet it has become one of the world's high-tech superpowers. Israel, dubbed "The Startup Nation," produces more startup companies per capita than any other country in the world. From military intelligence units to Google and Apple R&D centers, from traditional industries to artificial intelligence — this is a story of innovation born from necessity.

## The Funding Wave: From $5B to $24B

The past decade has revolutionized investment levels in Israeli high-tech. From $5 billion in 2015, total funding soared to a record $23.7 billion in 2021 — a nearly fivefold increase. Even after the 2022-2023 correction, levels remained significantly above the previous decade.

From the government's Yozma program to billion-dollar unicorns, Israeli high-tech has undergone an extraordinary journey. But challenges remain: social gaps between the center and periphery, a shortage of skilled workers, and dependence on foreign markets. Future success depends on maintaining the entrepreneurial spirit while broadening the circle — so that Israeli innovation becomes everyone's legacy.
