They live in Shanghai and Sao Paulo, in Marrakech and Melbourne, in Uman and Umm al-Fahm. 15.8 million Jews — less than a quarter of one percent of the world's population — are scattered across more than a hundred countries, yet they maintain a shared identity thousands of years old. The story of the Jewish diaspora is one of the most remarkable in human history: a story of exiles and redemptions, of loss and preservation, of a people who refused to disappear.
