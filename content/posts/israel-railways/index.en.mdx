From the first Ottoman locomotive that traveled from Jaffa to Jerusalem in 1892, to the high-speed train that now covers the same route in just 28 minutes — this is a story of dramatic transformation.

## Impressive Growth: From 25 Million to 70 Million

The most striking statistic about Israel Railways is its growth rate. In 2005, around 25 million passengers rode the train annually. By 2024, that number had nearly tripled — to 70 million. This is a growth rate that very few rail networks in the world have achieved, and it reflects a deep cultural shift in Israel's relationship with public transportation.

Prolonged lockdowns, public transit restrictions, and the shift to remote work brought the railway to its lowest point in two decades. </InfoCard>

The two flagship projects in the pipeline are the **Eastern Railway** — a new line running through eastern Gush Dan that will dramatically reduce commute times from the periphery to the center — and the **Rishon LeZion–Modi'in line**, creating a direct new connection between the two cities. These projects are expected to add tens of thousands of daily passengers and reduce congestion on major traffic corridors.
</InfoCard>

Furthermore, the full network electrification plan — expected to be completed by 2030 — will enable quieter, faster, and more environmentally friendly rail service. The transition from diesel to electric locomotives will reduce the railway's air pollutant emissions by approximately 80%.
